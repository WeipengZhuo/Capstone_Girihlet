Enabling discovery through effective and interactive distributed pipeline development






Major bottlenecks in genomic research:




Big Data

100s TBs of data per individual across time-points

Complex Pre-processing

Raw data pre-processing requires complex and generalized assumptions across samples

Inaccessible Parameters

Hidden and indecipherable - limits exploration and discovery



Objective:

Develop a platform to not only be able to pre-process a huge amount (~TBs) of genomic data but also give complete control and visibility of underlying process parameters to the end-point users.

Main Methods:


1. Effective Distributed Pipeline


How it works:

Raw genomic data are partitioned and distributed across worker nodes which processes independently and in parallel to extract only relevant sequences. These sequences are then aligned with reference genomes in distributed databases


Main benefits:

Efficient and scalable across distributed cloud platforms.

Fault tolerance due to redundant node partitioning.

2. Intuitive and Interactive Visualization

How it works:

Tableau Public is integrated with AWS Databricks to seamlessly feed analytic parameters, results, and graphs to Heroku for display and interactive manipulation of all stages


Main benefits:

Dashboard for parameter settings and results exploration.

Giving complete control to users.

Process result summary:


Results 1:

Pre-processed sequences shows binomial distribution in total sequence length which may indicate a bi-stabilizing length zones. This may be of interest to end-users


Results 2:

Pre-processed sequences shows normally distributed base-pair nucleotide ratio which is to be expected. However, the mean of the distribution is much higher than 0.5, which indicates A-T pairs are dominant in the pool of desired sequences. This should be interesting to investigate further to see why there is a biological bias in nucleotides used for these set of genes
